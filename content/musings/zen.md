## Principles:
- **Mutual arising** or the principle of **non-duality**. You cannot have a mountain with one side. You cannot have a wave that is only crest and no trough. You cannot have a front without back. There is no inside without an outside. You cannot have life without death. They go together. Our illusory perception of these phenomena as separate things/events is a result of our narrow view of reality (such as viewing a cat through a small hole would make us think of a separate head and tail that follow each other in time, rather than an integrated whole). 
- **Direct pointing**. This is how "teaching" is achieved in Zen tradition. Not through metaphor or allegory, but by directly pointing at the nature of reality and of one's mind. 
- The principle of spontaneity/naturalness/"non-stickiness"/"wu-wei"/not forcing/non-interference/going with the grain/going with the flow. 
	- Tacking against the wind while sailing. Judo. 

- - "Master, what is the essence of Zen?" - "When hungry, eat; when tired, sleep". - "But, isn't that what everyone does?" - "They do no such thing; when hungry, they don't just eat, but think of countless things; when tired, they don't just sleep, but dream innumerable dreams".
- "When sitting, just sit. When walking, just walk. But whatever you do, don't wobble. (unless wobbling is what you're doing)".
