Resilience is obviously important in life. But how can someone get it? 

I guess the only way is through hardship. There is no way to become resilient while being [[comfort-creep|comfortable]]. If everything is easy, then what is there to be resilient about? 

But how can we cultivate hardship in the modern world where most things come easily to us? One way is to go on outdoor adventures. Real adventures though, where there is some uncertainty remaining. I guess that resilience is about uncertainty as well as hardship. Another way is through physical effort that feels challenging. Another way is through mental effort that is challenging. 

Challenge, discomfort, uncertainty. All these things build resilience, but we have slowly built them out of our lives and the lives of our children. It is a normal human tendency to crave ease, comfort, certainty, since we "grew up" as a species in such a hostile environment (as did all the species, we're not special in that). And no matter how much of them we got, the balance was still towards their opposites. 

But not any longer. Now, we have engineered our lives in such a way that these adversities are largely absent. The adversities that remain are of a low-intensity, long-term stress sort. Precisely the sort that does not breed resilience, but helplessness and, when coupled with comfort in every other department, entitlement. 

Because, to become resilient, you would need short periods of intense effort or challenge, followed by periods of rest/consolidation, during which, either physically or mentally, you can recover and grow. Similar to what muscles would need during [[Modulation|recovery from training]].
