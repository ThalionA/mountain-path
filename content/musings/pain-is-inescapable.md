title: Pain is inescapable

By "pain" here I mean multiple kinds of physical or mental pain/discomfort. 

We face a given amount of pain throughout our lives. It's not possible to avoid all kinds of pain. Since the brain is good at normalising its inputs (see also [[comfort-creep|Comfort Creep]]), even if we devote our lives to the avoidance of any kind of discomfort, the brain will just lower the bar for what qualifies as uncomfortable. 

Even though we can't control the total amount of pain we face, we can "tune" to one kind of pain or another. For example, unless you are born with exceptional genetics, you will have to face the emotional pain of being bad at a given sport or the physical "pain" of the effort required to be good at it. You can either choose the pain of being overweight and having the resultant health effects or the pain of restricting your food intake in sensible ways. You can choose the pain of a bad career or the pain of [[Discipline|discipline]], hard work, etc. 

The point is that [[everything-is-a-trade-off|everything is a trade off]]. There are no free lunches. There is no avoiding pain, but we can choose which pain we prefer. Actually, when we accept that a certain amount of pain is guaranteed no matter what, then we can at least choose the pain that brings some good with it.  

A pertinent question is how to identify which kind of pain we'd prefer to live with in the long run. Does it have to be the case that we experience the pain of avoidance multiple times before we realise we'd rather have the pain of action? How, given humans' evolutionary propensity to discount the future, do we make sure that we take a long-enough view?

As always it must come back to individual [[values|values]]. In the end, you have to set your own objective function to optimise.
