title: Generalism vs specialisation

In "kind" environments (with repeatable situations, where feedback is quick), it's ok to be a specialist. In "wicked" environments (characterised by high uncertainty, with causal relationships that are hard to infer, with feedback being not dependable) it's better to be a generalist. (from the book [[Range - David Epstein|Range]])

At least in the beginning, it would be good to let children sample widely, sports, instruments, interests. Let them try things, discover what they enjoy and what they are naturally good at, and any disadvantage in terms of "headstart" can be overcome with adaptability and [[resilience|resilience]].
