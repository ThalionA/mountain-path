title: Effective work principles
description: A short list of practices I try to hold to. Not aspirational — what's actually worked across multiple seasons of intellectual work.
date: 2024-05-16
tags:

---

- Do not check email/slack often.
	- Instead batch-process once a day, later in the day if possible (not first thing in the morning).
- Try to focus on ONE task at a time.
	- Avoid task-switching (it takes a long time to get back to focused mode and increases stress and cognitive load).
- Write down details of unfinished tasks so that they do not take up cognitive resources.
- Good sleep and nutrition help with consistent energy levels throughout the day.
- Fighting against distractions is cognitively draining too.
	- The best is to design an environment free of them and/or condition yourself to not self-interrupt.
	- Discipline is not a good long-term strategy.
- Delete social media from phone.
- Have phone out of sight.
- Have lunch away from desk, outside if possible. Disconnect from work for a while.
- After lunch, take a short walk (even 10min is good).
- Identify the 1-2 most important things to accomplish on the evening before.
