This was partly spurred by Steven Pinker's *Enlightenment Now*. "The sin of ingratitude may not have made the Top Seven, but according to Dante it consigns the sinners to the ninth circle of Hell". There are myriads of aspects of my circumstances in life that I am grateful for (but even much less than I should be!). Honestly, the mind boggles at the number of things.

But what is the point of this list? It's not to self-congratulate or to be complacent. It's not even to feel guilty because of all the unearned boons and comforts I now enjoy. It's just to be happy. To take stock and appreciate all the things I can enjoy. Partly because one day I might not have them any longer, and it will be a small kindness to remember a time when I actually appreciated what I had. And partly because it can breed optimism. If we don't think that things are going well right now, we're also less likely to see how things might get even better in the future. And thus, we're less likely to take the actions that will lead us to that better future.

Here they are in an unordered list that will be incomplete and can be supplemented as time goes by:

- I am healthy. At this moment in time, extraordinarily healthy — not suffering from any genetic or acquired diseases. No infections, no cancers, no major injuries. I am even strong and fit quite a bit above average. I have access to good healthcare if something does go wrong.
- I am safe. I do not face any mortal danger from day to day.
- My family is also healthy and safe.
- I have access to clean water and food. Essentially unlimited amounts of both. So much, that it requires discipline to avoid eating *too much*. Such are our times, when more people die of metabolic disorders caused by taking in too many calories daily rather than too few.
- I live in a democratic country, with free speech, socioeconomic mobility, ability to come and go as I please.
- I can travel *by plane(!)* in a few hours to see my family in Greece, thousands of miles away, a journey that in past centuries would take months, and would likely result in death from hunger, disease, accident, or murder.
- I can buy almost whatever I want nearly instantaneously and have it *delivered* to my doorstep essentially the next day. This is crazy. Literally, no amount of money could buy this level of abundance in past ages, even if one were a king. Refrigerators, washing machines, etc etc etc did not even exist. As with food, we have the opposite problem now, having to fight consumerism, turning towards minimalism. These are really first-world problems.
- At any given moment, I have access to nearly all the hard-won knowledge that humanity acquired over its entire existence. All I have to do is flick my fingers, like a literal wizard. I can do this at home or at almost any random place.
- Apart from not having to fear death, hunger, thirst, and disease, I also don't have to face the *uncertainty* that my circumstances will change from one day to the next. I can count on the fact that I can pursue my goals today, tomorrow, and next year. Has personal agency ever been higher in history?
- I had the freedom of choosing my partner as I wished. This is not to say that I had free will in the matter, but I was not coerced by parents, state, church, tradition etc, as people would have been previously.
- In my work, I get to learn new and interesting things every day. I *get* to sit on my ass all day (ok, this is not necessarily the best for my health), instead of breaking my back in the fields like most of humanity since the agricultural revolution. I work with a flexibility that is unheard of. I work with (mostly) interesting, nice, intelligent people.
- I live in a beautiful city with so many options for things to do. Most importantly for me, I have easy access to hills for running and hiking.
- I have so much free time. I can spend it to my liking. I get to go bouldering several times a week. I play football once a week.
- I can sleep for 8h or more every single night.
- I can read almost any book ever written near instantaneously for a very small price. If I don't want to pay this price, I can even find it online for free. I can even listen to audiobooks for a very small cost while I cycle or drive.
- The maps are so good that I never get lost. The maps *predict* which route is best for me to take, so that I arrive in the shortest time at my destination.
- I have access to at least 3 computers at any time, more powerful than the ones NASA used to send men to the moon!!
- I can expect to live until 80 at least and for my children to live just as long.
- I can listen to any kind of music I want, anywhere, at any time.
- I can talk to my family, thousands of miles away, using audio and video, at any point at all.
- My Kindle Scribe. I can take as many notes as I want without being limited by paper, or weight, or light. I can keep them organised. I can have them whenever/wherever I want. I can turn them relatively easily to typed text.
- I can literally *see* inside another mammal's brain! How extraordinarily unimaginable would that have been to people of the past.
