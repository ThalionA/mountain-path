How do we know someone's potential? First, you would have to define in which area of life? Is it climbing? Is it science? Is it art? Is it driving? How is performance to be measured so that we know whether the potential has been reached or not?

When is someone's "absolute" potential defined? Is it when they're born? Is it in utero? Is it when the universe began? 

Given that there's no [[Free will|free will]], does it even make sense to think about "potential"? Isn't everything just what it is? If you made certain choices and you ended up performing at some level, it's not like you *could* have made different choices and performed better. So, is it the case that whatever we achieve in life has always *been* our actual potential? We never had more "latent" potential and it just *seemed* like we did?

But let's for a moment accept that "latent potential" exists for everyone and it is, at least in principle, achievable. Does *anyone* achieve their potential? Given that every minute choice you make on a daily, hourly, momentary basis has an outsize impact down the line, and given that the complexity of the world is so high that we can't actually predict the outcomes of each action, then there are infinitely more ways to *not* achieve our potential and maybe just *one* way in which to do so. Almost every choice we make further constrains the upper limit of what we can achieve. Even if someone, say Adam Ondra, climbs things that no one else has climbed before, did he really achieve his own true potential? Could he *really* not have made any single better decision during his life (e.g. one less bottle of beer per year, 5min more sleep on average every year - this is not a single action obviously, but still a single commitment/habit) that would've elevated his performance even more? Hardly. So it looks like even in the presence of free will, it's still impossible to reach some true latent potential.
