A phenomenon in which humans adapt to higher levels of comfort, with the effect being that contexts that would have been judged as comfortable previously are now judged as uncomfortable. Higher levels of "objective" comfort are required to maintain the same sense of comfort.

If you have a limited number of response levels (like a real biological neuron does), then you have "decide" how to best position this response range to "encode" a variable of interest. What if the variable of interest can take any arbitrary value that you can't predict a priori? Then you might observe which values it usually takes and then position your responses in the range of most frequently seen values. If the range of observed values changes, the positioning of your responses should change too, otherwise you're going to be left behind, responding at the same level irrespective of what the variable's value is (since it falls out of your range). Your actual response levels remain exactly the same (since they are constrained by evolutionary pressures), they are just placed in a different range of the variable. Therefore, you are not encoding absolute values of the variable, but relative changes of it.

In the same vein, if we have a finite ability to represent different levels of comfort, and if we want to keep being sensitive to changes in comfort levels, then we have to adapt our definitions or relative comfort and discomfort. The neutral point of neither comfort nor discomfort should correspond to the average level of comfort we have been experiencing over the last x amount of time. Hence, we over time get used to being more comfortable and previous conditions that would've seemed supremely comfortable may now be viewed as abhorrent (e.g. someone who grew up in a very poor environment and didn't have electricity half of the time, studies, moves to a city, gets a job at a tech company, and now if the internet connection is slightly slower for 2min throws a tantrum).

Adaptation is useful and necessary of course. However, the rates of adaptation are not necessarily symmetric in the two directions. We seem to adapt much faster to better, easier, more comfortable circumstances than to adverse ones. Why is it so?
