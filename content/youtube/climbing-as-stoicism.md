Climbing, like life, isn't always fair. As a shorter climber (165cm) I often find myself frustrated when taller climbers seem to have it easy — reaching through sequences I have to dyno for, locking off where I have to flag. The Stoic move is to notice the framing: "unfair" is the wrong frame, because difficulty is the whole point. If I wanted easy I wouldn't be here. The mental discipline is to redirect attention from things I can't control (height, ape index, route setting) to things I can (technique, strength, fear management, actually trying hard).
