Are you training hard, watching all the videos, and still not seeing the progress you want? The answers might be in a fifteen-year-old book. Dave MacLeod's *9 out of 10 climbers make the same mistakes* is, in my opinion, the best of the genre — and arguably more relevant now, in the deluge-of-information era, than when it was written. The three big takeaways I keep coming back to: most climbers are stuck on fundamentals while optimising details that don't matter, most climbers don't systematically push past their comfort zone, and most climbers are quietly limited by fear of falling without realising how much practice it actually takes to fix.
