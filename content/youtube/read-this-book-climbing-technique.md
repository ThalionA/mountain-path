The book club entry on John Kettle's *Rock Climbing Technique*. Most of us don't have a technique problem in the way we think we do — we have an *attention* problem. Kettle's framing of technique as something you practise by deliberately attending to specific aspects of your movement (rather than absorbing implicitly through volume alone) is one of the things that materially improved my climbing during my [[brachialis-tendon-recovery|brachialis recovery]], when I had to climb several grades below my max and had nothing to do except pay attention.
