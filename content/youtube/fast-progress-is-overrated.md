The question isn't how fast you can get better — it's why you want to. Fast-progress content sells because it's outlier content, but the outliers got there from factors you can't replicate (height, weight, ape index, starting age) and chasing their pace is mostly a recipe for overtraining, injury, and ego damage from constant comparison. Slower progress with proper motor learning principles produces longer-lasting skills, more generalisation, and far fewer brachialis injuries (see [[brachialis-tendon-recovery|how I learned that one the hard way]]). The question I'd leave you with: what if you were stuck at the same grade forever — would you keep climbing?
