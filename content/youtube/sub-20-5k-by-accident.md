I'd tried and failed to run a sub-20min 5K for years. The year I finally cracked it, I'd stopped training for it. The two accidental changes that made the difference: a much larger low-intensity aerobic base (a 25km cycle commute several days a week, mostly in zones 1–2), and a small dose of very high-intensity hill running in the Pentlands. Standard advice, finally applied — by accident.
