For two years I struggled to climb at my limit because of a brachialis tendon injury — originally from holding kickboxing pads for 100kg guys, then made dramatically worse by every habit a stressed PhD student could stack on top (skipped warmups, poor sleep, emotional eating, jumping onto limit problems from the desk, taking two weeks off after every flare-up only to do the same thing again). The turning point was a physio who told me to *not* stay away from the wall — instead warm up properly and do lots of low-intensity climbing, several grades below my max, plus simple band-resisted rehab exercises. The POLICE protocol (Prevention, Optimal Loading, Ice, Compression, Elevation) replaced the old RICE advice. Today I feel it in less than 1% of sessions. The three lasting changes: get smarter (sleep, nutrition, [[warmup-that-made-me-injury-free|warmups]]), get stronger (rehab → weighted pullups → [[one-arm-pullup-training|one-arm pullups]]), and get better (technique, especially via [[read-this-book-climbing-technique|John Kettle's book]]).
