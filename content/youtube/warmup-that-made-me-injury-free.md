We live in a time where everything has to be fast, including your progress in climbing, and we're bombarded with content telling us how things can be done in record times. This is the opposite argument: a 25–40 minute climbing-specific warmup that doubles as my supplemental strength training. Hammer curls, weighted pullups, max hangs on the fingerboard, interleaved with mobility work during the 5-minute rests. Two consequences of slowing down: my finger and elbow tendons stay healthy ([[brachialis-tendon-recovery|important context]]), and I bring a calmer, more focused state to the actual climbing — which matters more for hard sessions than I used to think.
