The progression that worked for me — regular pullups, added weight, isometric holds and lockoffs at the angles you're weakest, slow eccentrics, band- or pulley-assisted one-arm work, and scapular shrugs to bulletproof the supporting structures. The key step that most people skip is finding the *specific* weak point in your own lockout and training that range, rather than just adding load to the full pullup. Useful prerequisite work for [[brachialis-tendon-recovery|recovering from elbow tendon issues]] — building strength gradually across the full range was a big part of my long-term fix.
