> [!note] About these notes
> This was okay. Listened to as audiobook. Not too original, not too eye-opening, but some good reminders. Some takeaways below.

- There's a difference between fake bravado and real toughness.
- Distinguish between **building toughness** (teaching someone how to deal with adversity and training them incrementally) and **filtering** people (hit them with an extreme task so that only the "tough" remain — basically a form of survivorship bias).
- People who feel they have some control / autonomy can persevere longer in tough situations. Authoritative figures in coaching/management don't work.
- It's important to notice what you feel, be able to name it, understand the nuances.
- Mindfulness. Presence.
- Internal vs external motivation (internal leads to more perseverance).
- Elite runners pay attention to the signals from their body, whereas amateurs try to distract themselves.
