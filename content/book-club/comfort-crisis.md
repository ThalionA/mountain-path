> [!note] About these notes
> These are my reading notes for personal use, not a review. Listened as audiobook. Below is what stuck.

## Boredom

- People prefer getting electrocuted to being alone and bored for 15 minutes (men >> women).

## Nature

- 20 min in nature 3x per week.
- 5h in nature (a bit more wild than urban parks) per month.
- Japanese and Swedish studies of forest bathing.
- 3-day rule for being in the wilderness.
- Effects are counteracted by the presence of phones.
- Similar effects to meditation.

## Silence

- Noise levels are much higher in cities than they would be naturally.
- Man-made sounds have different statistics than natural sounds (much more repetitive), lead to more stress.
- Noise makes us stressed. Leads to higher incidence of heart disease.

## Hunger

- Thin people underestimate calories by ~250, overweight by ~720 kcal.
- Carb-fat-rich foods aren't naturally occurring.
- Real hunger now drives ~20% of eating.
- Stress drives a lot of the "comfort eating" (particularly chronic stress).
- Dieters who do not ban foods are less likely to binge.
- Least filling: croissant. Most filling: potato.
- Fewer high-caloric-density foods = better health.
- Non-linear weight gain (it happens around holidays or stressors).
- More snacking now. Eating window of 15h.
- 12–16h needed to fully metabolise the last meal and autophagy to begin. (Is eating after dark disruptive to circadian rhythms?)
- 24h fasts beneficial for health occasionally.
- Embrace the discomfort of hunger.

## Death

- "Case study" of Bhutan. Ranks highly in happiness/life satisfaction, very low in GDP. People have little debt, they own their houses, the majority live in small villages of ~200 people. Only 6% obese. Healthcare is free (if a procedure cannot be done in Bhutan, then they pay for it). Death is part of culture. Buddhism is the religion.
- Women in 1930s US had 36 clothing items on average. Now it's closer to 120.
- Improving our means becomes an end in itself to keep us distracted from our inevitable death. Busy-ness, hustle culture.
- Relevant: *The Denial of Death* (Ernest Becker), *Worm at the Core* (Sheldon Solomon).

## Movement

- Average humans today burn far fewer calories per kg of weight (they weigh quite a lot more) than prehistoric hunter-gatherers.
- Hunter-gatherers routinely walk/run more than 5 miles per day, even >20 miles on hunt days. They rest in squat positions.
- Upright position of humans confers an energetic advantage for carrying loads (quadrupeds can't really carry much).
- Humans are weak and slow. But they are good at endurance in hot weather (in cold weather it's sled dogs). And that's how they can run down prey (which they then have to carry back to camp).
- Soldiers carry 100 lb packs now, up from ~50 lb packs in WWI/II. Research shows up to 50 lb has good training effects without increasing chance of injury.
