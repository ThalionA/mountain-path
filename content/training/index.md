I train across several disciplines at once — alpine climbing and sport climbing as the apex, strength work and running as the substrate, football for the joy of it. The thread tying them together is that I treat the body as a system to be measured, modelled, and updated, not a vessel to be motivated.

This section publishes a curated subset of:
- **Analyses** — what the multi-year telemetry says about autonomic state, body weight coupling, strength and CNS, VO2max, sleep, perturbation events. Methodology and headline findings, with the most sensitive day-by-day data kept private.
- **Concepts** — things I've worked out about telemetry-driven loading, connective-tissue adaptation timelines, concurrent-training interference, and the like.
- **Logs** — periodic write-ups of training blocks, projects, and the occasional PR.

I don't publish the live decision system (the one that gates whether I should hit a max single tomorrow). That stays private, because mixing public commentary with operational decision-making degrades both. What does get published is the analytical layer underneath it.

## Operating principle

The data is the coach. Subjective "feels fine" is noise; rolling HRV, weight trends, sleep architecture, and recent output are signal. I try to be honest in writing about places where the data and my preferences disagree.
